← Back to Autumn Term

Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Create effective plot twists that surprise and engage readers.
  • Use foreshadowing and misdirection to set up plot twists.
  • Ensure plot twists are believable and enhance the overall story.

Topic Introduction

Plot twists are unexpected turns in the story that change the reader's understanding of events, characters, or motivations. Effective plot twists require careful planning, subtle foreshadowing, and believable execution. They should feel surprising yet inevitable, enhancing the story's impact rather than feeling contrived or unfair to the reader.

In-Class Activities

  • Analyse plot twists in famous stories and films to identify effective techniques.
  • Practice writing scenes with subtle foreshadowing that leads to twists.
  • Discuss what makes a plot twist satisfying versus frustrating.
  • Rewrite story endings to include effective plot twists.

Homework Assignments

  • Write a 400-word story that includes a plot twist.
  • Include subtle foreshadowing that makes the twist feel earned.
  • Ensure the twist changes the reader's understanding of the story.

Assessment Details

This week's assessment focuses on your ability to create and execute effective plot twists in narrative writing.

Assessment Questions

  1. Write a 500-word story that builds to a surprising plot twist.
  2. Include at least three pieces of subtle foreshadowing.
  3. Ensure the plot twist feels believable and enhances the story's impact.
