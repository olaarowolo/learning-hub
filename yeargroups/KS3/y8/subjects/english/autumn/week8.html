Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Use semi-colons correctly to separate independent clauses and items in complex lists.
  • Distinguish between when to use semi-colons, colons, and commas.
  • Apply semi-colon rules to improve sentence variety and clarity.

Topic Introduction

Semi-colons (;) are used to connect two independent clauses that are closely related in thought. They can also be used in complex lists to separate items that contain commas. Semi-colons provide a stronger break than a comma but weaker than a full stop, allowing writers to show relationships between ideas while maintaining sentence flow.

In-Class Activities

  • Identify correct and incorrect uses of semi-colons in model texts.
  • Practice combining independent clauses with semi-colons.
  • Create complex lists that require semi-colons for clarity.
  • Compare semi-colons with other punctuation marks and discuss their effects.

Homework Assignments

  • Write 8 sentences using semi-colons to connect related independent clauses.
  • Create 4 complex lists that use semi-colons to separate items.
  • Explain why a semi-colon is more appropriate than a comma or full stop in each case.

Assessment Details

This week's assessment focuses on your ability to use semi-colons correctly in different contexts.

Assessment Questions

  1. Write four sentences using semi-colons to connect independent clauses.
  2. Create two complex lists using semi-colons to separate items.
  3. Explain the difference between using a semi-colon and a comma in one of your examples.
