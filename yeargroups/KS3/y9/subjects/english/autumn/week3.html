Week 3 - Narrative Writing: Plot Development

← Back to Autumn Term

Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Structure narratives with clear beginning, middle, and end.
  • Develop engaging plot lines with rising action and conflict.
  • Use pacing techniques to maintain reader interest.

Topic Introduction

Plot development is the backbone of any narrative. A well-structured plot includes an engaging beginning that hooks the reader, a middle that builds tension through conflict and rising action, and an end that provides resolution. Effective plot development requires careful pacing, logical progression of events, and the ability to maintain reader engagement throughout the story.

In-Class Activities

  • Analyse plot structures in model narratives and identify key elements.
  • Practice outlining story plots using storyboards or plot diagrams.
  • Write opening scenes that immediately engage readers.
  • Develop conflict and rising action in short story scenarios.

Homework Assignments

  • Create a plot outline for a 500-word story including beginning, middle, and end.
  • Ensure your plot has clear conflict and rising action.
  • Write the opening paragraph that hooks the reader.

Assessment Details

This week's assessment focuses on your ability to develop engaging plot structures in narrative writing.

Assessment Questions

  1. Write a 300-word story opening that establishes setting, characters, and initial conflict.
  2. Outline the plot development for the rest of the story.
  3. Explain how your plot structure maintains reader interest.
