Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Identify and analyse themes and viewpoints in texts.
  • Use deductive reasoning to understand authorial intent.
  • Evaluate how different elements contribute to overall meaning.

Topic Introduction

Deduction involves using logical reasoning to reach conclusions about themes, viewpoints, and authorial intent. Unlike inference which focuses on implied meanings, deduction uses evidence from the text to draw logical conclusions about broader concepts like themes, character development, and the author's message or perspective.

In-Class Activities

  • Analyse short texts to identify main themes and supporting evidence.
  • Discuss how different viewpoints are presented and evaluated.
  • Compare themes across different texts or genres.
  • Debate the author's intent and how it's conveyed through language choices.

Homework Assignments

  • Read an article or story and identify 3 main themes.
  • For each theme, provide evidence from the text and explain its significance.
  • Discuss how the author's viewpoint influences the presentation of these themes.

Assessment Details

This week's assessment focuses on your ability to identify themes and analyse viewpoints in texts.

Assessment Questions

  1. Identify two main themes in the provided text.
  2. Provide evidence from the text to support each theme.
  3. Analyse how the author's viewpoint influences the presentation of these themes.
