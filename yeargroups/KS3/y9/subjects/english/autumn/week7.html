Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Use semi-colons to connect related independent clauses.
  • Use colons to introduce lists, explanations, and quotations.
  • Apply advanced punctuation to improve sentence clarity and flow.

Topic Introduction

Semi-colons and colons are powerful punctuation marks that create sophisticated sentence structures. Semi-colons connect two independent clauses that are closely related in thought, providing a stronger connection than a period but less separation than a comma. Colons introduce information that explains, illustrates, or amplifies what came before, such as lists, explanations, or quotations.

In-Class Activities

  • Practise converting comma splices into properly punctuated sentences using semi-colons.
  • Create sentences using colons to introduce lists and explanations.
  • Analyse model texts to identify correct usage of semi-colons and colons.
  • Edit sentences to improve clarity using advanced punctuation.

Homework Assignments

  • Write 5 sentences using semi-colons to connect related ideas.
  • Write 5 sentences using colons to introduce explanations or lists.
  • Ensure each use improves the sentence's clarity and sophistication.

Assessment Details

This week's assessment focuses on your ability to use semi-colons and colons correctly and effectively.

Assessment Questions

  1. Write three sentences using semi-colons to connect related independent clauses.
  2. Write three sentences using colons to introduce lists or explanations.
  3. Explain how these punctuation marks improve sentence clarity and flow.

Semi-colon: Connects two independent clauses that are closely related (e.g., "I love reading; it transports me to other worlds.").
Colon: Introduces a list, explanation, or quotation (e.g., "I need three things: patience, persistence, and practice.").
Use semi-colons instead of commas when ideas are too closely related for a full stop.
