Week 12 - Speaking & Listening: Debates

← Back to Autumn Term

Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Participate effectively in structured debates.
  • Use evidence and reasoning to support arguments.
  • Respond to counter-arguments with confidence and clarity.

Topic Introduction

Debates are structured discussions where participants present arguments for and against a topic. Successful debaters use clear reasoning, evidence, persuasive language, and active listening skills. This week focuses on developing these skills to help you articulate your views effectively and engage respectfully with opposing viewpoints.

In-Class Activities

  • Participate in mini-debates on current topics.
  • Practise rebuttals and counter-arguments.
  • Use evidence from research to support arguments.
  • Focus on clear speech, confident body language, and respectful responses.

Homework Assignments

  • Research a debate topic and prepare arguments for both sides.
  • Write a 300-word speech supporting one viewpoint.
  • Identify potential counter-arguments and prepare responses.

Assessment Details

This week's assessment focuses on your ability to participate effectively in a debate.

Assessment Questions

  1. Participate in a class debate on an assigned topic.
  2. Use evidence and reasoning to support your arguments.
  3. Respond effectively to counter-arguments from opponents.
