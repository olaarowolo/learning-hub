Year 6 English Curriculum Plan (UK National Curriculum)

Year 6 English focuses on consolidating reading, writing, grammar, and vocabulary skills to prepare pupils for secondary school. Pupils develop the ability to write with precision, analyse complex texts, and produce both creative and formal writing for a variety of purposes. Lessons include reading, discussion, research, drama, and structured writing tasks.

Autumn Term

Focus: Reading comprehension, narrative and paragraph writing, grammar consolidation

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – identifying main ideas, themes, summarising, and retrieval.
  • Weeks 3–4: Writing paragraphs and extended narratives; characterisation, setting, and plot development.
  • Weeks 5–6: Grammar – expanded noun phrases, complex sentences, conjunctions, relative clauses.
  • Weeks 7–8: Punctuation – commas in lists and clauses, apostrophes, inverted commas, dashes, brackets.
  • Weeks 9–10: Narrative writing – story openings, tension, climax, and resolution.
  • Weeks 11–12: Speaking and listening – presentations, debates, storytelling performances, drama activities.

Spring Term

Focus: Extended writing, non-fiction texts, comprehension, poetry

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – inference, deduction, analysis of author's viewpoint, comparing texts.
  • Weeks 3–4: Narrative writing with dialogue, paragraphing, and figurative language.
  • Weeks 5–6: Non-fiction writing – reports, biographies, persuasive texts, instructions, formal letters; use of headings and subheadings.
  • Weeks 7–8: Grammar – adverbs, prepositions, modal verbs, passive voice, formal vs informal tone, sentence types.
  • Weeks 9–10: Poetry – figurative language, rhythm, rhyme, performance, and writing original poems.
  • Weeks 11–12: Editing and proofreading – grammar, punctuation, spelling, sentence structure, vocabulary precision.

Summer Term

Focus: Creative writing, reading analysis, SATs preparation, vocabulary development

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension review – summarising, analysing author's techniques, evaluating texts.
  • Weeks 3–4: Writing imaginative stories – plot twists, complex characters, dialogue, and descriptive language.
  • Weeks 5–6: Non-fiction writing – newspaper articles, biographies, instructions, persuasive letters.
  • Weeks 7–8: Creative writing – descriptive passages, diary entries, formal writing, and speeches.
  • Weeks 9–10: Grammar and vocabulary – complex and compound sentences, varied punctuation, synonyms, antonyms.
  • Weeks 11–12: End-of-year assessment – reading fluency, comprehension, creative and non-fiction writing, grammar, punctuation, vocabulary, and SATs-style exercises.
