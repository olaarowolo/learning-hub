Lesson Note

Understanding Skip-Counting by 4

Skip-counting by 4 is counting forward in steps of 4. It helps you recognise patterns in numbers and is a foundation for multiplication by 4.

  • Example: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20 …
  • Notice the difference between consecutive numbers is always 4.
  • Try counting objects in groups of 4 to see this in real life.

Tip: Practice aloud and try writing your own sequences. This helps independent learning and memory.

Activity Overview

Practice skip-counting by 4. This strengthens number sense and prepares you for multiplication and division facts of 4.

Instructions

  • Count aloud in steps of 4 up to 100.
  • Fill in missing numbers on the sequences below.
  • Try real-life examples: count chairs in rows of 4, or sets of 4 pencils.
