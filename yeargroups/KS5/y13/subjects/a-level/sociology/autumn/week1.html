Learning Objective

Understand the concepts of crime and deviance, and the role of sociology in studying them.

Topic Introduction

Crime refers to acts that break the law, while deviance is behaviour that violates social norms. Sociology examines why people commit crimes and how society responds.

Key Terms

  • Crime: An act that violates criminal law.
  • Deviance: Behaviour that violates social norms.
  • Social Norms: Expected behaviours in society.
  • Criminal Justice System: Institutions that enforce laws.

In-Class Activities

  • Discussion: What is the difference between crime and deviance?
  • Group activity: Examples of deviant but not criminal behaviour.
  • Case study: Analyse a famous crime from a sociological perspective.

Homework Assignments

  • Read about the sociology of crime.
  • Write a short essay on why deviance exists in society.
  • Research one sociological theory of crime.

Assessment Details

End of week quiz on key concepts.

  • Question 1: Define crime and deviance.
  • Question 2: Give an example of deviance that is not crime.
  • Question 3: Explain the role of the criminal justice system.
