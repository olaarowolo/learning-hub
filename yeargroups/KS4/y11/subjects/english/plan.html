Year 11 English Curriculum Plan (by Week)

Year 11 English focuses on advanced reading, writing, speaking, and listening skills, preparing pupils for GCSE examinations. Pupils study fiction, non-fiction, poetry, and drama, developing analytical, creative, and formal writing abilities.

Autumn Term

Focus: Reading comprehension, analytical writing, grammar, and punctuation

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – inference, deduction, evaluating themes and viewpoints
  • Weeks 3–4: Narrative writing – plot, characterisation, setting, tension, and dialogue
  • Weeks 5–6: Grammar – complex sentences, relative clauses, conjunctions, stylistic devices, sentence variety
  • Weeks 7–8: Punctuation – semi-colons, colons, dashes, brackets, inverted commas, ellipses
  • Weeks 9–10: Analytical writing – responding to texts with evidence and evaluation
  • Weeks 11–12: Speaking and listening – presentations, debates, storytelling performances, exam-style discussions

Spring Term

Focus: Non-fiction writing, poetry, extended analysis, and exam skills

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – analysing author's techniques, language, structure, comparing texts
  • Weeks 3–4: Non-fiction writing – analytical essays, persuasive texts, formal letters, reports; focus on structure, audience, and purpose
  • Weeks 5–6: Narrative writing – descriptive and imaginative writing, dialogue, plot structure, character depth
  • Weeks 7–8: Poetry – analysis of poetic techniques, structure, tone, imagery, and creating original poems
  • Weeks 9–10: Grammar – advanced adverbs, prepositions, modal verbs, passive voice, sentence variety, stylistic devices
  • Weeks 11–12: Editing and proofreading – refining grammar, punctuation, vocabulary, sentence structure, and exam technique

Summer Term

Focus: Creative writing, analytical reading, GCSE exam preparation

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – evaluating texts, comparing authors' techniques, identifying themes, tone, and style
  • Weeks 3–4: Imaginative writing – narratives with plot twists, complex characters, dialogue, and descriptive language
  • Weeks 5–6: Non-fiction – formal reports, persuasive texts, analytical essays, letters, and speeches
  • Weeks 7–8: Creative writing – descriptive passages, diary entries, short plays, monologues
  • Weeks 9–10: Grammar and vocabulary – compound-complex sentences, figurative language, precise word choice, stylistic techniques
  • Weeks 11–12: Final GCSE-style assessment – reading fluency, comprehension, creative and non-fiction writing, grammar, punctuation, vocabulary, and analytical skills
