Topic: Reading Comprehension - Inference and Deduction

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the difference between explicit and implicit information in texts
  • Develop skills in making inferences based on evidence from the text
  • Practice deduction techniques to draw logical conclusions
  • Evaluate themes and viewpoints presented in literary and non-fiction texts

In-Class Activities

  • Group Discussion: Analyze a short extract from a novel, identifying explicit details and inferring character motivations
  • Pair Work: Read a newspaper article and deduce the author's viewpoint on a current issue
  • Whole Class: Examine a poem for themes of identity and belonging, discussing how language choices support inferences
  • Interactive Exercise: Use online tools to practice inference skills with timed quizzes

Homework

  • Read the provided short story extract and answer inference questions
  • Write a 200-word response explaining how the author's language creates a sense of mystery
  • Prepare for next week's discussion on themes in modern fiction

Section A: Inference from Fiction (15 marks)

Read the following extract from "The Curious Incident of the Dog in the Night-Time" by Mark Haddon:

"I know that people who work in offices get very upset when their computers don't work properly. I know that people who work in offices get very upset when their computers don't work properly. I know that people who work in offices get very upset when their computers don't work properly. I know that people who work in offices get very upset when their computers don't work properly."
  1. What can you infer about the narrator's personality from this repetition? (3 marks)
  2. Deduce why the narrator might be anxious about computers. (3 marks)
  3. How does this extract contribute to the theme of communication difficulties? (4 marks)
  4. Evaluate the author's viewpoint on neurodiversity. (5 marks)

Section B: Deduction from Non-Fiction (15 marks)

Read the following newspaper article excerpt:

"The government has announced a new initiative to reduce plastic waste in our oceans. Environmental groups have welcomed the plan, but critics argue it doesn't go far enough. The scheme will require supermarkets to charge for plastic bags from next year."
  1. What can you deduce about the public's attitude towards environmental issues? (3 marks)
  2. Infer the potential impact of this initiative on consumer behavior. (3 marks)
  3. Evaluate the author's viewpoint on government environmental policies. (4 marks)
  4. How might this article influence readers' opinions on plastic use? (5 marks)

Section C: Themes and Viewpoints (10 marks)

  1. Compare the themes of identity in two different texts studied this week. (5 marks)
  2. Discuss how an author's viewpoint can influence the presentation of themes. (5 marks)

Remember to support your inferences with evidence from the text. Good inferences are logical and based on clues provided by the author.
Deduction involves drawing conclusions from the information given, often requiring you to 'read between the lines'.
When evaluating themes and viewpoints, consider how the author's choices influence the reader's understanding.
