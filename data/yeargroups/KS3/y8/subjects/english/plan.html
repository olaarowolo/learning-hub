Year 8 English Curriculum Plan (UK National Curriculum)

Year 8 English focuses on advanced reading, writing, speaking, and listening. Pupils develop analytical and evaluative skills, write for a variety of purposes, and refine grammar, punctuation, and vocabulary. Lessons combine reading, discussion, research, drama, and extended writing tasks.

Autumn Term

Focus: Reading comprehension, narrative writing, grammar, and punctuation

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – summarising, inference, and identifying themes and ideas.
  • Weeks 3–4: Writing structured paragraphs and extended narratives; characterisation, setting, and plot development.
  • Weeks 5–6: Grammar – complex sentences, conjunctions, relative clauses, stylistic devices.
  • Weeks 7–8: Punctuation – colons, semi-colons, commas, dashes, brackets, inverted commas, ellipses.
  • Weeks 9–10: Narrative writing – tension, plot twists, climaxes, and resolutions.
  • Weeks 11–12: Speaking and listening – presentations, debates, role-play, storytelling performances.

Spring Term

Focus: Extended writing, non-fiction, comprehension, poetry, and analysis

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – analysing author’s purpose, viewpoint, language techniques, and comparing texts.
  • Weeks 3–4: Narrative writing – dialogue, paragraphing, figurative and descriptive language.
  • Weeks 5–6: Non-fiction writing – reports, persuasive texts, letters, instructions, articles; formal style and layout.
  • Weeks 7–8: Poetry – analysing figurative language, rhythm, rhyme, structure; composing original poems.
  • Weeks 9–10: Grammar – adverbs, prepositions, modal verbs, passive voice, sentence types, stylistic devices.
  • Weeks 11–12: Editing and proofreading – grammar, punctuation, vocabulary, sentence variety, and style.

Summer Term

Focus: Creative writing, reading analysis, vocabulary development, and GCSE preparation skills

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – evaluating texts, comparing author techniques, identifying themes and tone.
  • Weeks 3–4: Imaginative writing – complex narratives, character development, dialogue, setting, and plot arcs.
  • Weeks 5–6: Non-fiction – formal letters, persuasive writing, reports, and analytical responses.
  • Weeks 7–8: Creative writing – descriptive passages, diary entries, short plays, and monologues.
  • Weeks 9–10: Grammar and vocabulary – compound-complex sentences, figurative language, precise diction, and stylistic effects.
  • Weeks 11–12: End-of-year assessment – reading fluency, comprehension, creative and non-fiction writing, grammar, punctuation, vocabulary, and analysis.
