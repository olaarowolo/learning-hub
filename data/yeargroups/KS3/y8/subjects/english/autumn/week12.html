Week 12 - Speaking and Listening: Debates

← Back to Autumn Term

Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Participate effectively in structured debates using persuasive arguments.
  • Research and prepare evidence-based arguments for different viewpoints.
  • Use rebuttal techniques and respond to counter-arguments.

Topic Introduction

Debates are structured discussions where participants present arguments for and against a proposition. Effective debaters research their topics thoroughly, present clear arguments supported by evidence, and respond thoughtfully to opposing viewpoints. Good debating skills include active listening, critical thinking, and persuasive communication.

In-Class Activities

  • Participate in mini-debates on familiar topics to practice basic skills.
  • Learn debate structures and roles (proposition team vs opposition team).
  • Practice rebuttal techniques and responding to counter-arguments.
  • Analyse famous debates to identify effective argumentation strategies.

Homework Assignments

  • Research a debate topic and prepare arguments for both sides.
  • Write a 300-word argument supporting one viewpoint with evidence.
  • Prepare potential rebuttals to common counter-arguments.

Assessment Details

This week's assessment focuses on your ability to participate in a structured debate with well-researched arguments and effective rebuttals.

Assessment Questions

  1. Participate in a class debate on an assigned topic.
  2. Present clear arguments supported by evidence and examples.
  3. Demonstrate effective rebuttal skills when responding to opponents.

Structure your arguments: Claim (main point), Evidence (facts/examples), Reasoning (explanation of how evidence supports claim).
Use rebuttals effectively: Acknowledge opponent's point, then provide counter-evidence or show weakness in their argument.
Stay focused and respectful: Listen actively, avoid personal attacks, and address the arguments not the person.
