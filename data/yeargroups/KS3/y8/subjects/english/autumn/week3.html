Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Structure paragraphs with a clear topic sentence, supporting details, and concluding sentence.
  • Use appropriate linking words and phrases to connect ideas within and between paragraphs.
  • Develop paragraphs that are focused, coherent, and well-developed.

Topic Introduction

A well-structured paragraph is the building block of effective writing. Each paragraph should have a clear purpose and contain related ideas that support the main topic. The PEEL structure (Point, Evidence, Explanation, Link) is a useful framework for writing strong paragraphs that are focused and persuasive.

In-Class Activities

  • Analyse model paragraphs to identify their structure and effectiveness.
  • Practice writing PEEL paragraphs on given topics.
  • Peer review paragraphs for clarity, coherence, and development.
  • Discuss how linking words improve the flow of writing.

Homework Assignments

  • Write a structured paragraph (PEEL format) on a topic of your choice.
  • Include a topic sentence, supporting evidence, explanation, and linking sentence.
  • Use at least 3 different linking words or phrases.

Assessment Details

This week's assessment focuses on your ability to write well-structured paragraphs with clear organisation and appropriate linking.

Assessment Questions

  1. Write a paragraph explaining the importance of recycling in modern society.
  2. Ensure your paragraph follows PEEL structure with clear topic sentence, evidence, explanation, and link.
  3. Use appropriate linking words to connect your ideas smoothly.
