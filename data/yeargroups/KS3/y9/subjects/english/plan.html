Year 9 English Curriculum Overview

Year 9 English focuses on developing analytical, reading, writing, and speaking skills in preparation for GCSEs. Pupils study a broad range of fiction, non-fiction, and poetry texts, learning to write critically and creatively with clarity, accuracy, and style.

🎯 Core Aims

By the end of Year 9, pupils should be able to:

  • Read fluently and critically, analysing and evaluating fiction, non-fiction, and poetry.
  • Understand and explain authorial intent, themes, viewpoints, and language techniques.
  • Plan, draft, edit, and refine writing for different audiences and purposes, including formal and creative styles.
  • Use a wide range of punctuation accurately, including colons, semi-colons, dashes, brackets, ellipses, and inverted commas.
  • Apply grammar confidently: nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, conjunctions, prepositions, pronouns, modal verbs, relative clauses, passive/active voice, and sentence variety.
  • Develop a precise and sophisticated vocabulary; use context, dictionaries, and thesauruses.
  • Speak confidently in debates, presentations, and discussions, articulating ideas clearly and persuasively.

📅 Autumn Term

Focus: Reading comprehension, narrative writing, grammar, and punctuation

Weeks 1–2: Reading Comprehension

Inference, deduction, identifying themes and viewpoints.

Weeks 3–4: Narrative Writing

Plot, characterisation, setting, tension, and dialogue.

Weeks 5–6: Grammar

Complex sentences, relative clauses, conjunctions, stylistic devices.

Weeks 7–8: Punctuation

Semi-colons, colons, dashes, brackets, inverted commas, ellipses.

Weeks 9–10: Writing Extended Narratives

Climax, resolution, and suspense.

Weeks 11–12: Speaking and Listening

Presentations, debates, storytelling, and drama activities.

📅 Spring Term

Focus: Non-fiction writing, extended analysis, comprehension, and poetry

Weeks 1–2: Reading Comprehension

Analysing author techniques, comparing texts, evaluating viewpoints.

Weeks 3–4: Non-fiction Writing

Reports, persuasive writing, formal letters, articles; using formal tone, headings, and subheadings.

Weeks 5–6: Narrative Writing

Descriptive writing, dialogue, plot structure, character depth.

Weeks 7–8: Poetry

Analysis and creation; figurative language, rhyme, rhythm, tone, and imagery.

Weeks 9–10: Grammar

Adverbs, prepositions, modal verbs, passive voice, sentence variety, stylistic devices.

Weeks 11–12: Editing and Proofreading

Refining grammar, punctuation, vocabulary, sentence structure, and style.

📅 Summer Term

Focus: Creative writing, analytical reading, vocabulary development, and GCSE preparation

Weeks 1–2: Reading Comprehension

Evaluating texts, analysing themes, tone, style, and author's purpose.

Weeks 3–4: Imaginative Writing

Narratives with plot twists, detailed characters, dialogue, and advanced descriptive language.

Weeks 5–6: Non-fiction Writing

Formal reports, persuasive texts, analytical essays, and letters.

Weeks 7–8: Creative Writing

Short stories, diary entries, monologues, and descriptive passages.

Weeks 9–10: Grammar and Vocabulary

Compound-complex sentences, figurative language, precise word choice, and stylistic techniques.

Weeks 11–12: End-of-year Assessment

Reading comprehension, creative and non-fiction writing, grammar, punctuation, vocabulary, and analytical skills.

📊 Assessment Overview

  • End of Autumn Term: Reading comprehension, narrative writing, grammar, punctuation, and vocabulary.
  • End of Spring Term: Extended writing, non-fiction texts, poetry, comprehension, and analytical skills.
  • End of Summer Term: Mastery check – reading fluency, comprehension, creative and non-fiction writing, grammar, punctuation, vocabulary, and preparation for GCSE-style tasks.

✏️ Example Weekly Detail

Week 4 (Autumn) – Writing Detailed Narratives

Learning objective: Write a narrative with well-developed characters, plot, dialogue, and figurative language.

Activity ideas:

  • Analyse model texts for structure, tension, and descriptive language.
  • Plan narratives using storyboards or paragraph planners.
  • Peer assessment – evaluate paragraphs for clarity, style, grammar, and vocabulary.

Practice Questions:

  • Write a scene where a character faces a difficult choice.
  • Include dialogue, at least one simile or metaphor, and varied sentence structures.
  • Ensure accurate punctuation and paragraphing.
