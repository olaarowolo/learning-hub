Year 10 English Curriculum Plan (UK)

Year 10 English focuses on developing analytical, creative, and formal writing skills, alongside critical reading and speaking skills. Pupils study a range of literature, non-fiction, and poetry texts, learning to write with clarity, style, and precision, preparing them for GCSE examinations.

Autumn Term

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – inference, deduction, identifying themes, motifs, and viewpoints.
  • Weeks 3–4: Narrative writing – plot, characterisation, setting, tension, and dialogue.
  • Weeks 5–6: Grammar – complex sentences, relative clauses, stylistic devices, sentence variety.
  • Weeks 7–8: Punctuation – colons, semi-colons, brackets, dashes, inverted commas, ellipses.
  • Weeks 9–10: Extended narratives – climax, resolution, and suspense.
  • Weeks 11–12: Speaking and listening – presentations, debates, and storytelling performances.

Spring Term

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – analysing author’s purpose, viewpoint, and language techniques.
  • Weeks 3–4: Non-fiction writing – analytical essays, persuasive writing, formal letters, reports; focus on audience and purpose.
  • Weeks 5–6: Narrative writing – descriptive and imaginative writing, dialogue, paragraphing, figurative language.
  • Weeks 7–8: Poetry – analysis of poetic techniques, structure, rhyme, rhythm, and writing original poetry.
  • Weeks 9–10: Grammar – adverbs, prepositions, modal verbs, passive voice, stylistic and rhetorical devices.
  • Weeks 11–12: Editing and proofreading – improving grammar, punctuation, sentence variety, vocabulary, and style.

Summer Term

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – summarising, evaluating texts, comparing author techniques and viewpoints.
  • Weeks 3–4: Imaginative writing – narratives with plot twists, complex characters, dialogue, and descriptive language.
  • Weeks 5–6: Non-fiction – analytical essays, persuasive texts, formal reports, speeches, and letters.
  • Weeks 7–8: Creative writing – descriptive passages, diary entries, monologues, and short plays.
  • Weeks 9–10: Grammar and vocabulary – compound-complex sentences, figurative language, precise word choice, stylistic techniques.
  • Weeks 11–12: End-of-year assessment – reading fluency, comprehension, creative and non-fiction writing, grammar, punctuation, vocabulary, and analytical skills.
