Lesson Note

Understanding Skip-Counting

Skip-counting is counting forward by numbers other than 1. It helps with multiplication, division, and recognising patterns.

  • Counting by 2: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 …
  • Counting by 5: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25 …
  • Counting by 10: 10, 20, 30, 40, 50 …

Tip: Count aloud and look for the difference between numbers. Try real-life examples like counting coins or steps!

Activity Overview

Practice skip-counting by 2s, 5s, and 10s. This skill helps build strong foundations for multiplication tables.

Instructions

  • Count aloud in steps of 2, 5, and 10 up to 100.
  • Fill in missing numbers on number lines.
  • Try real-life skip-counting (e.g. counting coins, fingers, or groups of objects).
