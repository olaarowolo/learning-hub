Lesson Note

Understanding Skip-Counting Sequences

Skip-counting sequences help you recognise patterns and relationships between numbers.

  • Example: Counting by 3: 3, 6, 9, 12, 15 …
  • Sequences can start from any number and follow a consistent step.
  • Try making your own sequences with different step sizes to practice independent learning.

Tip: Write down sequences and check for patterns to strengthen number sense.

Activity Overview

Practice completing number sequences with different step sizes. This improves recognition of arithmetic patterns and prepares you for multiplication and division.

Instructions

  • Look at the starting number and step size.
  • Fill in the missing numbers in each sequence.
  • Try creating your own sequences for practice.
