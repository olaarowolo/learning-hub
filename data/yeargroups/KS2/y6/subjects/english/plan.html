year 6 English Curriculum Plan (UK National Curriculum - KS3)

year 6 English builds on KS2 skills, introducing KS3 expectations for reading, writing, and spoken language. Pupils read a range of texts, develop their writing for different purposes, and begin to analyse language and structure more deeply.

Core Aims

By the end of year 6, pupils should be able to:

  • Read and understand a range of fiction and non-fiction texts.
  • Write effectively for different audiences and purposes, using a range of structures.
  • Use grammar and vocabulary accurately to enhance writing.
  • Speak and listen confidently in formal and informal contexts.
  • Begin to analyse texts, identifying language techniques and author's intent.
  • Produce creative writing with developing characters and plots.

Autumn Term

Focus: Reading fiction and narrative writing

  • Weeks 1–2: Introduction to KS3 English – reading comprehension and annotation.
  • Weeks 3–4: Reading fiction – understanding plot, character, and setting.
  • Weeks 5–6: Narrative writing – developing stories with structure.
  • Weeks 7–8: Descriptive writing – using language for effect.
  • Weeks 9–10: Creative writing projects – short stories and poems.
  • Weeks 11–12: Assessment and review – reading and writing skills.

Spring Term

Focus: Poetry and spoken language

  • Weeks 1–2: Poetry analysis – structure, rhyme, and imagery.
  • Weeks 3–4: Writing poetry – original poems and forms.
  • Weeks 5–6: Drama and performance – scripts and role-play.
  • Weeks 7–8: Spoken language – presentations and discussions.
  • Weeks 9–10: Media texts – analysing advertisements and short films.
  • Weeks 11–12: Revision and assessment – poetry and speaking skills.

Summer Term

Focus: Non-fiction and language study

  • Weeks 1–2: Non-fiction texts – reading and evaluating articles.
  • Weeks 3–4: Expository writing – reports and explanations.
  • Weeks 5–6: Grammar and vocabulary – building accuracy.
  • Weeks 7–8: Persuasive writing – arguments and opinions.
  • Weeks 9–10: Creative non-fiction – biographies and travel writing.
  • Weeks 11–12: End-of-year assessment – comprehensive review.

Example Weekly Detail

Week 3 (Autumn) – Understanding Character in Fiction

Learning objective: Analyse how authors develop characters in stories.

Activity ideas:

  • Read character descriptions from novels and identify traits.
  • Discuss how characters change throughout the story.
  • Write a character profile with physical and personality traits.

Practice Questions:

  • Describe a main character's appearance and actions.
  • Explain how dialogue reveals character personality.
  • Predict how a character might develop in the story.

Assessment

  • End of Autumn Term: Reading comprehension and narrative writing.
  • End of Spring Term: Poetry and spoken language assessment.
  • End of Summer Term: Non-fiction writing and language skills.
