Topic: Order of Operations (BIDMAS) & Mixed Practice

Introduction

It's time to bring all four operations together! This week, we learn the universal rule for the order of operations: BIDMAS. This will allow you to solve complex calculations correctly and confidently. We'll consolidate all the skills you've learned so far.

Key Concepts Covered

  • Applying BIDMAS: Correctly use the order of Brackets, Indices, Division, Multiplication, Addition, and Subtraction.
  • Solving Complex Calculations: Tackle problems that include brackets, powers, and a mix of operations.
  • Consolidation: Practice using all four operations (+, -, √ó, √∑) together.
  • Real-Life Problem-Solving: Apply your skills to practical scenarios like budgeting and planning.

In-Class Activities

Let's put BIDMAS into action:

  • "BIDMAS Pyramid": A puzzle where you fill in the missing numbers in a pyramid by applying the order of operations.
  • Group Investigation: "Plan a Party": Work in a team to plan a party within a ¬£100 budget, requiring careful calculation of costs.
