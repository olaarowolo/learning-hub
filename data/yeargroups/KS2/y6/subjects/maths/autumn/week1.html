Weeks 1-2: Place Value, Rounding, Negative Numbers

← Back to Autumn Term

Topic: Place Value to 10 Million; Rounding to Specified Accuracy; Negative Numbers

Introduction

This week focuses on understanding place value up to 10 million, rounding numbers to different degrees of accuracy, and working with negative numbers in context.

Place value is the value of each digit in a number based on its position. Rounding helps us estimate and simplify numbers. Negative numbers are used for temperatures, debts, and more.

Tasks

Practice reading and writing large numbers, rounding to the nearest 10, 100, 1000, etc., and understanding negative numbers on a number line.

📊 Place Value to 10 Million

Place value shows the value of each digit in a number. For example, in 1,234,567:

  • 7 is in the units place (1)
  • 6 is in the tens place (10)
  • 5 is in the hundreds place (100)
  • 4 is in the thousands place (1,000)
  • 3 is in the ten thousands place (10,000)
  • 2 is in the hundred thousands place (100,000)
  • 1 is in the millions place (1,000,000)

🔹 Rounding to Specified Accuracy

Rounding makes numbers easier to work with. Look at the digit to the right of the place you're rounding to:

  • If it's 5 or more, round up.
  • If it's less than 5, round down.

Examples:

  • Round 1,234 to the nearest 100: 1,200
  • Round 5,678 to the nearest 1,000: 6,000

🔹 Negative Numbers

Negative numbers are less than zero. They are used for temperatures below zero, debts, etc.

On a number line, negative numbers go to the left of zero.

Examples:

  • -5 + 3 = -2
  • The temperature is -10°C.

📝 Practice Exercises

  1. Write 2,345,678 in words.
  2. Round 7,891 to the nearest 100.
  3. What is the place value of the digit 5 in 1,234,567?
  4. Calculate -4 + 7.
  5. Round 9,876 to the nearest 1,000.
