year 6 Mathematics Curriculum Plan (by Week)

Autumn Term

  • Weeks 1–2: Place value to 10 million; rounding to specified accuracy; negative numbers.
  • Weeks 3–4: Formal methods for addition/subtraction with large numbers.
  • Weeks 5–6: Long multiplication and short/long division; interpreting remainders.
  • Weeks 7–8: Factors, multiples, primes, highest common factors (HCF), lowest common multiples (LCM).
  • Weeks 9–10: Order of operations (BODMAS), multi-step reasoning problems.
  • Weeks 11–12: Revision, skills consolidation, summative assessment.

Spring Term

  • Weeks 1–2: Fractions—equivalence, improper fractions, mixed numbers.
  • Weeks 3–4: Add/subtract fractions with different denominators.
  • Weeks 5–6: Multiply/divide fractions and decimals; connect to percentages.
  • Weeks 7–8: Decimals—place value, ordering, operations.
  • Weeks 9–10: Percentage equivalents of fractions/decimals; percentage problems.
  • Weeks 11–12: Measures—conversion (length, mass, volume), compound shapes (perimeter and area).

Summer Term

  • Weeks 1–2: Geometry—angles at a point, on a straight line, in triangles/quadrilaterals.
  • Weeks 3–4: Position and direction—coordinates (1st quadrant), translations, reflections.
  • Weeks 5–6: Statistics—pie charts, line graphs, data interpretation.
  • Weeks 7–8: Volume and capacity—estimating and calculating (e.g., cubes, cuboids).
  • Weeks 9–10: Mixed problem-solving with real-life contexts (fractions, percentages, measures).
  • Weeks 11–12: Year-end review, SATs preparation, summative assessment.
