Topic: Comparing Size and Mass

Introduction

This week, we will explore the concepts of size and mass. Children will learn to compare objects and use language like 'bigger', 'smaller', 'heavier', and 'lighter'.

Tasks

Practice comparing everyday objects. Use a balance scale to compare the mass of different items.

üìä Comparing Size and Mass

Size is how big or small something is. Mass is how heavy it is. We can use words like big, small, long, short, tall, heavy, and light.

üîπ Comparing Size

Look at two objects and decide which is bigger or smaller.

Examples:

  • A bus is bigger than a car.
  • An apple is smaller than a watermelon.

üîπ Comparing Mass

Hold two objects to feel which is heavier or lighter. You can use a balance scale to check.

Examples:

  • A book is heavier than a pencil.
  • A feather is lighter than a stone.

üìù Practice Exercises

  1. Find something bigger than your hand.
  2. Find something smaller than your shoe.
  3. Which is heavier, a toy car or a teddy bear?
  4. Which is lighter, a leaf or a rock?
  5. Order three different-sized balls from smallest to largest.
