Year 2 English Curriculum Plan (UK)

Year 2 English focuses on consolidating reading, writing, speaking, and listening skills while developing more complex sentence structures, comprehension, grammar, and spelling. Lessons remain practical, visual, and interactive, incorporating phonics, storytelling, role-play, and creative writing.

Autumn Term

Focus: Reading fluency, sentence structure, grammar foundations

  • Weeks 1–2: Phonics revision; recognising common exception words.
  • Weeks 3–4: Reading short stories; sequencing events; retelling orally.
  • Weeks 5–6: Writing simple and compound sentences; capital letters, full stops.
  • Weeks 7–8: Introduction to adjectives and verbs in sentences.
  • Weeks 9–10: Story writing – beginning, middle, end; punctuation review.
  • Weeks 11–12: Speaking & listening – show-and-tell, oral presentations, class discussions.

Spring Term

Focus: Comprehension, extended writing, grammar

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension – answering who, what, where, when questions.
  • Weeks 3–4: Writing narratives with clear sequencing; using “and,” “because.”
  • Weeks 5–6: Non-fiction writing – labels, lists, instructions.
  • Weeks 7–8: Introduction to past and present tense; correct verb usage.
  • Weeks 9–10: Poetry – rhymes, simple descriptive poems.
  • Weeks 11–12: Editing skills – checking spelling, punctuation, and sentence structure.

Summer Term

Focus: Consolidation, imaginative writing, reading comprehension, grammar

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension review; summarising stories.
  • Weeks 3–4: Writing short stories and letters; correct use of capital letters and full stops.
  • Weeks 5–6: Writing instructions and reports; sequencing ideas logically.
  • Weeks 7–8: Creative writing – character descriptions, imaginative settings.
  • Weeks 9–10: Grammar and vocabulary – adjectives, adverbs, nouns, verbs; conjunctions “and,” “but,” “so.”
  • Weeks 11–12: End-of-year assessments – reading fluency, comprehension, and writing skills.
