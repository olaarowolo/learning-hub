Year 1 English Curriculum Plan (UK)

Year 1 English focuses on developing reading, writing, speaking, and listening skills. Lessons should be engaging, practical, and multisensory, with plenty of story-telling, role-play, phonics practice, and writing activities.

Autumn Term

Focus: Phonics, early reading, sentence writing, speaking and listening

  • Weeks 1–2: Phonics (Phase 3) – letter sounds and blending for reading.
  • Weeks 3–4: Reading simple sentences; recognising high-frequency words.
  • Weeks 5–6: Writing simple sentences; capital letters, full stops.
  • Weeks 7–8: Story retelling; sequencing events.
  • Weeks 9–10: Speaking and listening activities – show-and-tell, class discussions.
  • Weeks 11–12: Introduction to descriptive vocabulary and simple question marks.

Spring Term

Focus: Developing fluency, comprehension, and sentence structure

  • Weeks 1–2: Phonics (Phase 4–5) – blending more complex sounds.
  • Weeks 3–4: Reading short stories; answering comprehension questions.
  • Weeks 5–6: Writing sentences with conjunctions (“and”) and adjectives.
  • Weeks 7–8: Retelling stories orally and in writing; sequencing paragraphs.
  • Weeks 9–10: Non-fiction texts – writing labels, lists, and captions.
  • Weeks 11–12: Introduction to past tense; simple editing skills.

Summer Term

Focus: Consolidation, imaginative writing, punctuation, and grammar

  • Weeks 1–2: Reading comprehension review; discussing favourite stories.
  • Weeks 3–4: Writing short narratives; using full stops, capital letters, question marks.
  • Weeks 5–6: Writing instructions; beginning to structure paragraphs.
  • Weeks 7–8: Poetry and performance – reciting and writing simple poems.
  • Weeks 9–10: Grammar and vocabulary review; adjectives, verbs, nouns.
  • Weeks 11–12: End-of-year writing assessment and reading fluency check.
